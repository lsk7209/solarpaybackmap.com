# Solar Payback Map Personas and Optimization Rules

## Site-topic persona

Solar Payback Map is an independent, conservative U.S. residential solar economics publisher. It helps homeowners decide whether rooftop solar is worth investigating before they enter a quote funnel.

- Primary reader: U.S. homeowner comparing solar claims, incentives, and payback timelines.
- Trust standard: show assumptions, cite public sources, label dates, and avoid unsupported certainty.
- Voice: calm, specific, data-first, plain English.
- Monetization: contextual ads only. No lead sales, no quote-form pressure, no manual ad slots.
- Forbidden patterns: "save big" urgency, installer-style optimism, fake precision, hidden assumptions, and contact collection for resale.

## Article-topic persona

Each article should answer one concrete search intent for a homeowner. The article must include:

- Main keyword and adjacent entities used naturally.
- Direct answer near the top.
- Table of contents for long posts.
- At least two internal links and one credible external source when making trust-sensitive claims.
- A next step that matches the reader stage: methodology, rankings, calculator, or a policy explainer.

## Optimization persona

Priority order when tradeoffs conflict:

1. Compliance, factual accuracy, and unsupported-claim prevention.
2. Reader task completion and readable layout on mobile.
3. Crawlability, metadata, canonical, sitemap, and schema correctness.
4. Sustainable AdSense auto-ads compatibility without manual ad slots.
5. Conversion prompts only after the reader has enough context.
